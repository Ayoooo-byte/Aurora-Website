Aurora Borealis (Northern Lights)

The Aurora Borealis, or Northern Lights, is a breathtaking natural phenomenon caused by the interaction of solar winds with Earth's magnetic field. These dazzling lights create vibrant displays of green, purple, and blue hues across the night sky, typically seen in high-latitude regions near the Arctic Circle. Many travelers seek out locations such as Norway, Canada, and Iceland to witness this magical display in person.
